A visualization of the U.S. affordability crisis.

Created by Matthew Pearson

Wages vs Housing Prices

This chart compares median household income (blue line) to median home sale prices (red line) over time.

Notice how since the 1980s, housing prices have risen much faster than wages, widening the affordability gap.

This trend has made homeownership increasingly difficult for many Americans.

Unemployment Rates for College Graduates

This chart shows unemployment rates for college educated individuals ages 20-24 (blue line) compared to the overall rate of unemployment, nationwide (red line).

The unemployment rate of college students and recent graduates fluctuates greatly by season, but historically oscillating around the overall rate of unemployment.

However, since the COVID-19 pandemic, the unemployment rate for college students and recent graduate age 20-24 has consistently stayed above the overall rate of unemployment. This indicates that recent graduates are facing more challenges in finding employment compared to the general population.

The Mountain of Debt

This chart illustrates the rising levels of different types of debt in the United States over time.

Credit card debt (yellow line), car loan debt (orange line), and student loan debt (blue line) have all seen significant increases, contributing to the overall financial burden on individuals and families.

The growth in these debts highlights the challenges many face in managing their finances amidst rising costs of living and education.

Why Does This Matter?

The widening gap between wages and housing costs, coupled with rising debt levels and challenging employment prospects for recent graduates, paints a concerning picture of financial stability in the U.S.

These factors contribute to increased financial stress for many Americans, making it harder to achieve economic security and long-term prosperity.

Understanding these trends is essential for policymakers, educators, and individuals alike to address the root causes and work towards solutions that promote financial well-being. Addressing these issues is crucial for ensuring that individuals and families can achieve economic security and prosperity.

This project was created as part of Critical Web Design (DIG 245) at Davidson College to shed light on these important issues and encourage informed discussions about economic policies and personal finance strategies.
